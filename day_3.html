ELON MUSK

"it is possible for ordinary people to choose to be extraordinary"



  • Elon Musk, a visionary entrepreneur, is transforming the world through innovation. From founding Tesla, SpaceX, Neuralink, and X (formerly Twitter), to reshaping energy, AI, and space travel—his bold ideas continually push the boundaries of science and technology. Now, as he takes steps toward forming a political party in the U.S., Musk proves that for him, even the impossible is just the starting point.

Here is a timeline of Elon Musk's life events:

  • 1971 – Born on June 28 in Pretoria, South Africa.
  • 1981 – At age 10, developed a passion for computing and self-learned programming.
  • 1983 – Created and sold a video game, Blastar, for $500 at age 12.
  • 1988 – Left South Africa for Canada to avoid mandatory military service.
  • 1989 – Enrolled at Queen’s University in Ontario to begin higher education.
  • 1992 – Transferred to the University of Pennsylvania; earned degrees in Physics and Economics.
  • 1995 – Dropped out of Stanford’s PhD program after just 2 days to pursue entrepreneurship.
  • 1995 – Co-founded Zip2, a software company for business directories and maps.
  • 1999 – Compaq acquired Zip2 for $307 million; Musk received $22 million from the sale.
  • 1999 – Founded X.com, one of the first online banks.
  • 2000 – X.com merged with Confinity to become what we now know as PayPal.
  • 2002 – eBay bought PayPal for $1.5 billion; Musk personally made $165 million.
  • 2002 – Founded SpaceX, aiming to make space travel more affordable and accessible.
  • 2004 – Invested in and became chairman of Tesla Motors.
  • 2006 – Co-founded SolarCity, a solar energy company later acquired by Tesla.
  • 2008 – Became CEO of Tesla during the global financial crisis; also launched the Tesla Roadster, the company's first production car.
  • 2010 – Tesla went public, becoming the first U.S. car company to IPO since Ford.
  • 2012 – SpaceX made history by sending the Dragon capsule to the International Space Station.
  • 2013 – Introduced the concept of the Hyperloop, a new high-speed transport system.
  • 2015 – Co-founded OpenAI to develop safe and beneficial artificial intelligence.
  • 2015 – Launched Model X, a high-performance electric SUV with falcon-wing doors.
  • 2016 – Founded Neuralink, a company working on brain-machine interface technology.
  • 2016 – Started The Boring Company to build tunnel infrastructure and reduce urban congestion.
  • 2017 – Introduced the Tesla Model 3, a mass-market electric vehicle that boosted EV adoption.
  • 2018 – Sent a Tesla Roadster into space aboard the Falcon Heavy rocket, symbolizing ambition and innovation.
  • 2020 – Tesla became the world’s most valuable car company; Musk rose to become the world’s richest person.
  • 2021 – SpaceX launched the Inspiration4 mission—the first all-civilian spaceflight.
  • 2022 – Acquired Twitter for $44 billion and rebranded it as X, signaling a move toward an “everything app.”
  • 2023 – Launched xAI, his AI company to build artificial general intelligence aligned with humanity.
  • 2024 – Announced plans to form a new political party in the U.S., aiming to influence national discourse and policy.
